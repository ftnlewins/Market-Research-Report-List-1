<p><p>The long-grain rice seeds market primarily consists of two types: Japonica and Indica rice. Japonica rice, typically short and plump, is favored in East Asian cuisines and known for its sticky texture when cooked, making it ideal for sushi and rice bowls. In contrast, Indica rice features long, slender grains that remain separate when cooked, making it popular in South Asian and Middle Eastern dishes. Both types cater to distinct culinary preferences and play crucial roles in global rice production and trade.</p></p>
