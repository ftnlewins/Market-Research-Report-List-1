<p><ul><li>Microparticulated Starch</li><li>Modified Starch</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The starch-based fat replacer market consists mainly of microparticulated starch and modified starch. Microparticulated starch is created by processing starch into small particles that mimic fat's texture, providing a creamy mouthfeel in low-fat products. Modified starch, on the other hand, is chemically or physically altered to enhance its thickening, gelling, or stabilizing properties, making it suitable for various food applications. Both types help reduce calories and improve the nutritional profile of food without sacrificing taste.</p></p>
