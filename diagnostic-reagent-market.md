<p><p>The diagnostic reagent market is divided into two primary types: in vivo and in vitro diagnostic reagents. In vivo diagnostic reagents are used to detect diseases or conditions within a living organism, often involving imaging techniques or biological markers. In contrast, in vitro diagnostic reagents are utilized for analyzing samples taken from the body, such as blood or tissue, outside the organism. Both types are essential for accurate diagnosis, monitoring of health conditions, and guiding treatment decisions.</p></p>
