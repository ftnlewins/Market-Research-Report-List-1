<p><p>Pet external fixation is a veterinary orthopedic procedure used to stabilize fractures and bone abnormalities in pets, particularly in dogs and cats. This method involves the application of external devices, such as pins and rods, that support and stabilize the affected bones without the need for invasive surgeries. Its advantages include reduced recovery time and lower complications compared to traditional internal fixation techniques.</p><p>The Pet External Fixation Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.30% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing pet ownership trends, rising awareness of veterinary healthcare, and advancements in fixation technologies.
