<p><ul><li>Air-cooled</li><li>Transport-cooled</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hydrogen fuel cell market is categorized into air-cooled and transport-cooled systems. Air-cooled fuel cells utilize ambient air for cooling, making them simpler and often more lightweight, suitable for stationary applications like backup power. In contrast, transport-cooled fuel cells use advanced cooling methods to manage heat in high-performance environments, such as vehicles, where efficiency and longevity are crucial. Both types are essential in advancing hydrogen technology, catering to different applications and operational demands in the growing clean energy sector.</p></p>
