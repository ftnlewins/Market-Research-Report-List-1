<p><p>Laser Direct Structuring (LDS) technology utilizes specific resins to create conductive pathways on three-dimensional surfaces for electronic applications. The market types include PA (polyamide) for flexibility and durability, PC (polycarbonate) for impact resistance, ABS (acrylonitrile butadiene styrene) for strength, and PC/ABS for enhanced performance. PPA (polyphthalamide) offers high thermal stability, while LCP (liquid crystal polymer) delivers excellent chemical resistance. Other materials, like PBT (polybutylene terephthalate) and COP (cyclo-olefin polymer), also contribute to specialized applications in electronic components.</p></p>
